# People

You need 1 game master or mistress and up to 60 players. The game is divided into 10 geographical regions, each with 6 roles. 

### Decisions are made by people or the app

In each region, **either all** ministries must be assigned to human players, **or none**. In other words, there cannot be a region where only 4 ministries are assigned to human players - or 3, or 5, and so on. So you need 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48, 54 or 60 players, because each region must be played *either entirely by humans* or *entirely by the app*. So if you have a number of people that is not divisible by 6, you should assign 2 people to some roles.  

The roles played by the app automatically make random political decisions, where the bottom 10% and the top 10% are cut off: if a policy has a range from 0 to 100, then the decision is randomly chosen to be between 10 and 90. 

If the game rounds are played on different days, the same people do not have to be present, but you must have the **same number of people** so that all roles originally assigned to human players will continue to be played by human players. 

The regions are: 

1. China 
2. Africa, south of the Sahara 
3. Middle East and North Africa 
4. Latin America 
5. Pacific Rim (countries bordering the Pacific: Canada, Japan, New Zealand, Australia, South Korea) 
6. South Asia (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh) 
7. Southeast Asia 
8. Eastern Europe and Central Asia 
9. The USA 
10. Europe  

Each region has the following roles: 

1. Minister for the Future 
2. Minister against Poverty 
3. Minister against Inequality 
4. Minister for Equality (Empowerment) 
5. Minister for Food and Agriculture 
6. Minister for Energy 

and a game organiser, in a school setting, this is the teacher.
