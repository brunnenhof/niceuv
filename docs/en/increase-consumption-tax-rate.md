# Increase consumption tax rate

This policy is an increase in the consumption tax (aka sales tax or value added tax (VAT),  etc. 0 means no increase, 10 means an increase by 10 percentage points, 5 by 5 percentage points; the money raised goes to general government revenue. The government can then use this extra money to [invest in new capacity or subsizise peoples' expenses](https://www.economicshelp.org/blog/glossary/difference-between-consumption-and-investment/). 

Note that raising consumption taxes across the board hurts poor people more than rich people: see [here](https://www.europeandatajournalism.eu/cp*data*news/how-vat-hinders-effective-redistribution-of-wealth/) for the gory detail.
