# Policy Sliders
Each ministry, except the Ministry for the Future, decides via several policies how much to invest in the future to improve the lives of the people in their region. For each policy you choose how much you invest by setting a slider somewhere between its minimum and maximum value. Each policy also has a drop-down that you can open for a short explanation of what the policy is and what it does. Finally, each policy has an entry in this help manual with more information.

Investments cost money. How much, the Minister for the Future can tell. He or she sees in real time the cost implications of each decision (slider move) the Minister for each portfolio takes. The Minister for the Future also sees how much the sum of all investment plans of his or her portfolio minister in their region are. The game app automatically compares this total with the maximum budget (= 2% of the GDP of the region) the region has to invest. If the total regional investment plans are below the budget, then the Future Minister can submit the porposals to the game organizer. 

!!! Note
    Once he or she does this, the portfolio ministers may still adjust their slider settings, but those changes are no longer taken into account for the current round!

What do the minimum and maximum values mean? That depends on the variable. Let's take as an example **Electrify everything**, a policy that the Minister for Energy has to decide. [See also the glossary entry](electrify-everything.md). **Everything** in this context means all the energy use currently still fueled by fossil fuels: cars, trucks, trains, planes and ships still using petrol or diesel; heating with oil or gas; industrial processes like making cement. As Minister for Energy, you can decide that you want to replace all this fossil fuel with electriciy. By how much? The minimum is set at 0, if you choose this, you actually don't change anything, you leave things as they are. The maximum is set at 95%, because there will be some uses of oil, gas or coal that cannot be replaced by electricity for technical or physical resons.

## The Cost of Investment
Investments cost money: When you set the slider at its minimum, you spend **nothing**. If you set the slider at its **maximum**, you spend the entire cost of that policy. If you set it anywhere in between, you spend the **prorated** cost. Prorated? If you set the slider at exactely half between min and max, you spend half the cost, set it at 2/3 between min and max, you spend 2/3 of the cost, and so on.

### Maximum cost
The **maximum** cost of each policy is calculated as follows: One of the original developers of the simulation model behind the game, Per Espen Stoknes, estimated that each **turnaround** (these are the Ministries in the game) for [Giant Leap](two-scenarios.md) would cost, world-wide, 1 Tera Dollar per year for Energy, Food & Agriculture, Empowerment and Poverty each; and 4 Tera Dollar per year for Inequality. Tera = 10^12 = 1 000 000 000 000. The GDP of the world is about 120 Tera Dollars in 2025. So the turnarounds cost a maximum of 8/120 = 7 % of the world's GDP. 

!!! Bonuspoints
    Can you find reliable data what it would cost your generation if the world decided to do **nothing**?

Now, **cost** is a misleading label, because this money does not disappear into thin air. It is instead **invested**, i.e. spent on innovation, re-training, building new things, wages for workers, etc. Still, if it is spent on Giant Leap, it is for starters not available for something else, hence people think of it as a cost. 

### Distribution of global costs across regions
In the game we take this global *cost* and distribute it over the 10 regions, based on the regions' relative weight of GDP. This means that a poor region pays less for a poverty (and any other) turnaround than a rich region. The reason is that in a rich region wages are higher, rents are higher, food cost is higher, etc. The next step in calculating the cost of a policy is to divide the *cost* for each entire turnaround by the number of policies in that turnaround (= ministry). This is, admittedly, a rough estimate, if you have a better idea, please send an email to <simfuture@blue-way.net>.

### Two modifications
At last, we then modify this cost by two influences: 

- 1) the environmental damage in the region - the **higher** the damage, the **higher** the investment cost. The logic here is that, for example, extreme weather doesn't occur just once every hundred years, but more like once every 10 years! Thus, you have to clean up and repair more often, build stronger houses, better roads and rail... 
- 2) the reform willingness in the region - the **more** willing, the **less** the investment cost. If people are more willing to try new things, the less you have to spend because thing get done faster, with fewer objections, and with more people working together, rather than against each other.
