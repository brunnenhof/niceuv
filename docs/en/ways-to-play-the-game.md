# Ways to play the game

With this game you can explore some of the big issues of our time: poverty, inequality, equal rights, food and agriculture, and energy, in a **playful** manner.

Here are some suggestions for how you may emphasize different gameflows through the game for the players. You can, for example, use it to

- [let players learn about the UN Sustainable Development Goals](let-players-learn-about-the-un-sustainable-development-goals.md),
- [engage players in conversation, discussion and negotiation](engage-players-in-conversation-discussion-and-negotiation.md),
- [let players **experience** differences in power](let-players-experience-differences-in-power.md),
- [lead players from one simple causal feedbackloop to a systems understanding](lead-players-from-one-simple-causal-feedbackloop-to-a-systems-understanding.md),
- or, perhaps best of all, do nothing: start the game and then stand back. Let your players play and watch what they do with the freedom that was given to them.
