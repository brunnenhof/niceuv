# Fraction of credit with private lenders NOT drawn down per year

Governments, and you, have a [creditworthiness](https://www.experian.com/blogs/ask-experian/what-is-creditworthiness/). For you, look at the link. For governements this is usually a multiple of the economic output ([GDP](gdp.md)). In reality, past repayment history also matters, and there, countries differ (if this interests you, a great book on this is [This time is different](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691152646/this-time-is-different)). 

Given your creditworthiness, you need to decide how much you want to *draw down* that credit you have. For example: If your creditworthiness is 100 million Dollars, you may want to *draw down*, i.e. borrow, half of that (= 50 million Dollars). Or 30% (= 30 million Dollars). Or nothing. 

Why would you not borrow everything? Because once you borrowed, you have to start repaying and you have to pay interest. Second, the bigger your debts, the lower your creditworthiness later on. It is usually the interest payments that make governments think twice about borrowing. In the US, for example, the [interest payments are (in 2026)](https://www.pgpf.org/programs-and-projects/fiscal-policy/monthly-interest-tracker-national-debt/) the third largest expense item in the budget, before, for example, spending on the military. 

The more you borrow, the higher the interest payment, the less you can spend on other things! So, set your policy wisely.
