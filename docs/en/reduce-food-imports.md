# Reduce food imports

[Much of the food we eat is imported](https://openknowledge.fao.org/server/api/core/bitstreams/7ca7c051-6ad2-4595-820b-c373fdb365f1/content/state-of-agricultural-commodity-markets/2024/pattern-evolution-food-trade.html), where even the [**net** exporters do import food](https://www.statista.com/chart/35606/main-food-products-exported-and-imported-by-the-united-states/) - they just export more than they import. 

You can change this by reducing imports in your region. That does not mean your people will go hungry, it just means that food will be produced locally and that less grain, pigs, chicken, tomatoes, cows, etc. will be shipped half way around the world. 

If you set the policy to 0, you reduce nothing, i.e. food will be exported and imported as now.  10=10% reduction, 50=50% reduction (the maximum).
