# LPB: Split the use of funds from public lenders

A further decision you need to take after you [get money from public lenders](lending-from-public-bodies-lpb.md) either [as loan or grant](lpb-funds-given-as-loans-or-grants.md), is what you will use the money for. 

There are two possible uses: You can invest the money, or you can consume it. Investing means spending the money on education, on security, on rail and roads, on health care, in other words, on things that last and need to be handled on the level of society. Consuming means spending money on transfers to poor people, on subsidizing (= supporting financially) rents, fuel, farmers, low income earners. This is often money that goes to individual people. 

The difference between investing and consuming is that investing usually has a long term benefit - when you learn today, you can apply what you learned for a long time. When you *invest* in your health by jogging a bit every day, you will benefit again for a long time. Consuming is done here and now and there is, usually, no long term benefit. A bit like the saying about giving people fish to eat (consumption) and teaching them how to fish (investing).

In this policy you can set the slider to 0 (= all conumption) or 100 (= all investing) or anywhere in between.  

Note: Setting this slider only makes sense if you set the [Lending from public bodies](lending-from-public-bodies-lpb.md) policy to something other than 0.
