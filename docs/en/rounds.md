# Rounds

### Three rounds of play

The game consists of 3 decision rounds. At the start of the game, information is displayed in graphic form (see [How do I read a graph?](how-do-i-read-a-graph.md)) from 1990 to 2025 for each region and each ministry.

### New information

When there is new information, the players should look at and discuss the results, first in their region and then across regions. For example, all energy ministers could meet and discuss energy issues, all poverty ministers could meet and discuss poverty issues, etc.

### Review finished

When the review of the past is complete, players must make decisions relevant to their respective ministry. Each minister is shown several investment possibilities that he or she can make. Each investment measure is briefly explained and they can decide, in consultation with their fellow ministers, to invest a certain amount to hopefully improve the lives of the citizens of their region. He or she determines the amount to be invested by moving a slider.

### Discussions and negotiations, face to face

Each measure carries an investment costs and the Minister of the Future looks at the total amount that his or her regional colleagues intend to invest. If this amount is below the budget (which is shown to the Minister of the Future), everything is fine and he or she can submit the decisions for the region for the current round. However, if the total amount of the investment plans exceeds the budget, the ministers in the region must renegotiate their plans until the total amount is within the budget. Only then can the Minister for the Future submit the decisions. **These discussions and negotiations are at the heart of the game**, forcing players to argue face-to-face in favour of their decisions, weigh up the pros and cons of a particular investment plan and compromise in order to move forward.

Once all regions (played by human players) have submitted their decisions, the game organizer lets the game continue.

### The time horizons 

The first round, in which the decisions are made in the current year, runs until the year 2040 - so it is the **future of the players themselves**.

The second round, in which the decisions are made in 2040, runs until 2060 - it is therefore the **future of the players' children**.

The third and final round, in which the decisions are made in the year 2060, runs until the year 2100 - it is therefore the **future of the players' grandchildren**.
