# Worker reaction

In any economy, the national income is shared between owners and workers. The sharing is done through negotiation between the two. Take a look at the share going to workers (= labor) in the US. Since 1948, the share has been going down and down and down. 
 ![fred-graph-of-us-labor-share-1948-2016](../assets/images/fred-graph-of-us-labor-share-1948-2016.png)
The [source of this image is here](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labor_share). 
Currently it is here:
![fredgraph](../assets/images/fredgraph.png) 
The situation is similar in other countries. What do the graphs tell you?

You can give the workers (= the ones who barely live from paycheck to paycheck) a lift: Another way to give workers a lift, is to **increase the workers reaction** to any demands the owners make. This gives workers more bargaining power vis-a-vis the owners - they become tougher. The policy you set is a percentage *increase* in the bargaining power. 0 leaves things as they are, you change nothing. 1 increases the power by 1%, 2 by 2% and 3 by 3%. This may not sound like much but it a start to address [inequality](inequality-and-social-tension.md).
