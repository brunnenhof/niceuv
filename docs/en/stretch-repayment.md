# Stretch repayment

When you borrow money, you agree to pay back the loan with a certain interest rate over an agreed time. For a consumer loan (for example for a vacation) this is usually a year or two. For a house, this might be as long as 30 years. The **longer** the repayment duration, the **lower** your periodic payment (with the same amount borrowed). Thus, when you are short of money, you may want to stretch your repayment. From 3 to 5 years, or from 20 to 30 years. 

This is the policy that lets you do that. Setting it to 1, the default, leaves things as they are. Setting it to 2 doubles your repayment duration, etc.
