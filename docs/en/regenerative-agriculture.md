# Regenerative agriculture

[We are loosing ground](https://www.bbc.com/future/bespoke/follow-the-food/why-soil-is-disappearing-from-farms/) - the land we need to grow our food. And [we spend ridiculous amounts of energy to get our food](the-energy-cost-of-feeding-the-world.md). 

But you can do something about it: with this policy you decide the percentage of your cropland that is worked regeneratively (=[low or no tillage](https://www.climatefarmers.org/blog/till-or-no-till-farming-opening-up-the-debate/), [low or no fertilizers and pesticides](https://www.bbc.com/future/bespoke/follow-the-food/the-clean-farming-revolution/)  etc.)  50 means 50% cropland worked is regeneratively, 100 = 100% of cropland is worked regeneratively, etc. 0 leaves things as *industrial* they are.
