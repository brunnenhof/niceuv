# Lending from public bodies (LPB)

Governments can borrow from private and public lenders. Public lenders are, for example, the [Worldbank](https://www.worldbank.org/ext/en/home), the International Monetary Fund, [regional Development Banks](https://www.bmf.gv.at/en/topics/budget-economic-policy/International-Financial-Institutions/Regional-Development-Banks.html). Very often, loans from these bodies are better: less interest to pay and longer time to repay. Sometimes the money is given as grants, i.e. no interest at all and no repayment ever. You can set the policy if it is given as grant [here](lpb-funds-given-as-loans-or-grants.md). 

In the game, you can borrow from these public lenders depending on the strength of your economy. At most, you can borrow 30% of your economic output ([GDP](gdp.md)). So, if your GDP is 50 billion Dollars per yer, you can borrow up to 15 billion Dollars. Of course, you can borrow less, or nothing at all!

Note: In this policy, as in any other policy where you **get** money ([raising taxes](increase-consumption-tax-rate.md), [taxing the rich](taxing-owners-wealth.md), [Universal Basic Dividend](introduce-a-universal-basic-dividend.md), etc.) you still have to **pay** an investment cost. 

Take 'taxing the rich' as an example: Just read any news anywhere in the world and you see how fierce the opposition to taxing the rich is. All kinds of stories are told: that they will leave the country when someone tries to tax them, that they create job after job so the poor can earn money, that their wealth will *trickle down* to the poor, eventually. To counter such entrenched opposition, you need to tell a different story, tell it often, get people to re-tell it, and, the hardest things of all, get them to believe it; all that costs money. Hence, you need to **spend**, long before you **get**. What are the stories told against raising taxes, against a Universal Basic Dividend, in your country?  Are they true? Do you believe them?
