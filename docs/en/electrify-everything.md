# Electrify everything

Our lives run on fossil fuels: oil, gas, coal. Burning those fuels sends CO2 into the atmosphere, and once there, warms [life on earth](https://wmo.int/publication-series/state-of-global-climate/state-of-global-climate-2025). One of the better ways to reduce global warming, and its disruptive effects on human life, is to use electric energy (from wind, solar, geothermal sources) instead of fossil energy. With this policy, you decide how much of fossil energy is replaced with electric energy for mobility (cars, trucks, trains, planes, ships), heating and industrial processes (like [making cement](https://www.checkatrade.com/blog/how-to/make-cement/)).

The maximum you can choose is 95 %, because there are some processes or uses that cannot be 'electrified' for technical or physical reasons.
