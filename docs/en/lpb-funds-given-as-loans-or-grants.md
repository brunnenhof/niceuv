# LPB: funds given as loans or grants

Once you [borrow money from public bodies](lending-from-public-bodies-lpb.md), you can set a secondary policy, namely whether the money you get is given to your government as [grant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grant_(money)) or as [loan](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wSqtotgaNAc). Loans have to be paid back, over time, and you have to pay interest to the lender. 

Say you borrow 100 Euros, for 3 years, with an interest of 3% per year. If you repay once a year, you have to pay 35.25€ each year, for a total of 106.06€. So the interest is 6.06€. You can do your own calculations [here](https://www.calculator.net/loan-calculator.html?cloanamount=100&cloanterm=3&cloantermmonth=0&cinterestrate=3&ccompound=annually&cpayback=year&x=Calculate&type=1#monthlyfixedr).

This policy lets you decide if all the money given to you is a loan (set the slider to 0) or a grant (set the slider to 100), or anywhere between. 50 means half as loan and half as grant, 30 means 30% as loan and 70% as grant.

Note: Setting this slider only makes sense if you set the [Lending from public bodies](lending-from-public-bodies-lpb.md) policy to something other than 0.
