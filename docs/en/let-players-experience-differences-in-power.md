# Let players experience differences in power

The best learning takes place not in your head, but when you have an [emotional moment of realization](https://www.reddit.com/r/movies/comments/11p019d/what*are*some*of*the*best*examples*of*moments_of/). Games are great settings for such moments. 

One we have used several time is to set up the room according how rich the regions are: rich regions have tables, comfortable chairs, space, light, snacks, fresh fruit... poor regions have no or tiny tables, uncomfortable chairs, little space, no fruit, stale snacks, and are placed in a dark corner. 

Four points:

1. Be very careful with this power stratification, it can go terribly wrong. 
1. Do a proper debrief about this power stratification with lots of time. 
1. In your debrief, address how the players during the game reacted to this: 
    1. did they accept it - the poor and the rich? 
    2. did they quietly equalize the situation? 
    3. did they even notice? 
    4. ...  
4. If in doubt don't do it!
