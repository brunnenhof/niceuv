# Strengthen Unions

In any economy, the national income is shared between owners and workers. The sharing is done through negotiation between the two. Take a look at the share going to workers (= labor) in the US. Since 1948, the share has been going down and down and down. 
 ![fred-graph-of-us-labor-share-1948-2016](../assets/images/fred-graph-of-us-labor-share-1948-2016.png)
The [source of this image is here](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labor_share). 
Currently it is here:
![fredgraph](../assets/images/fredgraph.png) 
The situation is similar in other countries. What does the graph tell you?

You can give the workers (= the ones who barely live from paycheck to paycheck) a lift: First, you can strengthen unions and union legislation. This gives workers a bigger share of the national income. The policy you set is a percentage *increase* in the share. 0 leaves things as they are, you change nothing. 1 increases the share by 1%, 2 by 2% and 3 by 3%. This may not sound like much but if you increase the workers' share from 50% to 51% (which an increase of 2 % is), you have at the same time reduced the owners' share from 50% to 49%. You've begun to tip the balance.
